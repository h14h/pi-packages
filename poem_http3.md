# Ode to HTTP/3

Beneath the web you browse each day,
A quiet revolution spins—
Not TCP, the old-school way,
But something sleeker now begins.

The queues of old would stall and choke,
One lost packet stopped the line,
While HOL blocking went up in smoke,
And latency refused to decline.

Enter QUIC, the bold and new,
Built on UDP's light embrace—
No three-way handshake slow as glue,
No waiting for a handshake's grace.

Streams now flow both fierce and free,
No head-of-line to block the stream,
Each request rides independently,
Like runners in a shared-lane dream.

And TLS? It's woven in,
Not layered on as afterthought—
Encryption from the very start,
Is baked right in, not tacked or bought.

Zero-RTT on return trips,
A warm hello to sites you know,
Resuming sessions fingertip,
With secrets from long ago.

Connection IDs hold the thread,
When IP addresses shift and roam,
Your session lives though networks fled,
A mobile phone still finds its home.

The frames now ride in binary,
Not text that wastes the wire's space,
More compact, swift, and canary—
A leaner, meaner, faster pace.

So here's to HTTP/3, the third,
The protocol that breaks the mold,
Where every packet keeps its word,
And latency is worth its gold.

The web evolves, the layers turn,
From HTTP/1's long-lived embrace,
Through spdy streams and HTTP/2's churn,
To QUIC's unyielding, faster pace.
